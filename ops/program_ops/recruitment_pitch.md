# AI Ninja Program — Cohort Recruitment

> Internal pitch for engineering managers and regional leaders

---

## The Opportunity

AI has become the dominant theme in every cybersecurity sales conversation. Customers ask about it daily — in RFPs, in architecture reviews, in executive briefings. They want to know how AI detects threats, how it differs from rules-based detection, whether it actually works or is just marketing. Most of our security architects and engineers cannot answer these questions beyond what is on the slide deck. When a competitor's SE can explain how their anomaly detection model works and ours cannot, we lose credibility — and we lose deals.

The AI Ninja Program fixes this. It is a 13-week, cohort-based training program that takes security professionals from zero AI/ML knowledge to building and demonstrating AI-powered security tools. By graduation, participants can explain AI to a CISO, dismantle a competitor's AI claims with technical depth, and pull out a laptop to demo a working security assistant. This is not a webinar series or a slide library. It is hands-on, assessed, and culminates in a live capstone presentation to stakeholders.

The program is selective — 12 to 16 participants per cohort — and structured for working professionals: approximately 5 hours per week of self-paced learning and coding, plus one hour of live session time. No prior AI or data science experience is required. Participants just need to be comfortable writing Python and willing to commit to the full 13 weeks.

## Who Should NOT Apply

Being honest about fit protects both the participant and the program:

| Situation | Why It Doesn't Work |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Cannot commit 5 hours per week consistently | The program is cumulative — falling behind by more than one week makes it very difficult to recover, and incomplete participants lower cohort energy for everyone |
| Looking for a certificate without doing the work | There are 4 assessed gates with real passing criteria; 67 hands-on coding exercises; and a live capstone demo in front of stakeholders — this is not a checkbox exercise |
| Does not write Python at all | Every week includes coding. Participants who cannot write a Python function on day one will spend all their time fighting the language instead of learning the concepts |

## Questions?

**"What if they fall behind?"**
All live sessions are recorded. Each participant is assigned a cross-region pair partner who checks in weekly. If someone falls more than one week behind, the facilitator works with them on a fast-track path. Each assessment gate can be rescheduled once (within a 2-week retake window).

**"What if they're in a different time zone?"**
The core curriculum is entirely self-paced — no fixed daily schedule. Live sessions are held at times that accommodate Americas time zones (ET/CT/PT/BRT), and all sessions are recorded for async access. Multiple time slots are considered when cohort composition is known.

**"Is this just another training that goes nowhere?"**
No. There are 4 graded assessment gates with real passing criteria. The final gate is a live capstone demo presented to a review panel of stakeholders — it simulates presenting AI capabilities to a customer. Certification is tracked, and Tier 3 graduates earn a recognized internal designation. The program also produces a tangible output: a portable demo kit every graduate can use in customer meetings immediately.

**"What's the cost?"**
This is an internal program with no external licensing or vendor fees. The only cost is the participant's time: approximately 5 hours per week for 13 weeks. There are no travel requirements — everything is delivered remotely.

**"What do they walk away with, specifically?"**
Every graduate receives: a tiered certification, a complete code portfolio (67 exercises + 4 capstone projects), a packaged customer demo kit (the RAG security assistant with curated threat data and a presentation script), an AI vocabulary reference mapped to security contexts, and access to the alumni network for ongoing knowledge sharing.

**"Can they do this alongside their normal workload?"**
Yes — the program is designed for it. The 5 hrs/week is a realistic average across the 13 weeks. There are no mandatory daily blocks. Participants manage their own schedule around customer commitments, with the only fixed time being the 1-hour weekly live session (which is recorded if they miss it).
