## Exercise 1: The Elevator Pitch (5 min)

Write your own 30-second explanation of "What is AI in cybersecurity?" in plain language. No jargon. Imagine you are explaining it to a CISO who is technically strong but has never studied ML.

**Constraints:**
- Maximum 3 sentences
- Must mention what makes it different from rules
- Must include one concrete security example

## Exercise 3: The 5-Question Framework (15 min)

Apply the AI Claim Evaluation Framework to these real-world vendor statements. For each, answer the 5 questions and rate the claim.

### Claim A
> "Our endpoint platform uses advanced AI to detect and prevent sophisticated threats in real-time, leveraging our proprietary machine learning models trained on trillions of events."

1. What model? 
2. What data? 
3. What metric? 
4. Compared to what? 
5. Where does it fail? 
**Rating:** Real / Partial / AI Washing

### Claim B
> "Our AI-powered email security solution uses natural language processing to understand email context and detect business email compromise with 99.7% accuracy."

1. What model? 
2. What data? 
3. What metric? 
4. Compared to what? 
5. Where does it fail? 
**Rating:** Real / Partial / AI Washing

### Claim C
> "We've integrated AI into our firewall. Our AI automatically learns your network patterns and adapts rules in real-time."

1. What model? 
2. What data? 
3. What metric? 
4. Compared to what? 
5. Where does it fail? 
**Rating:** Real / Partial / AI Washing

**Discussion:** Claim A has genuine technical substance but is vague on specifics. Claim B names a technique (NLP) and a metric (99.7%) but doesn't say what "accuracy" means in context (is it precision? recall? on what test set?). Claim C is the most suspect — "learns patterns" and "adapts rules" could mean anything from a real ML model to a threshold that adjusts over time.

## Exercise 4: Round-Table — AI Claims From the Field (15 min)

Each participant shares one AI claim they have personally encountered — from a competitor datasheet, a customer RFP response, a vendor demo, or their own product marketing.

**Format per claim (3 minutes each):**
1. State the claim (30 sec)
2. Group applies the 5-question framework (2 min)
3. Group votes: Real / Partial / AI Washing (30 sec)

**Facilitator note:** Encourage participants to share claims they are genuinely uncertain about, not obvious examples. The goal is to practice evaluation on borderline cases.

## Self-Study Reflection Questions

If working through this session independently, write brief answers to these:

1. Before this session, how would you have explained AI in security? How would you explain it now?
2. Which domain in the security stack (endpoint, network, email, identity, SIEM, cloud) do you think benefits most from AI? Why?
3. Think of a product you've worked with that claims to use AI. Can you answer all 5 evaluation questions about it? What's missing?
4. If a customer said "AI is just marketing hype," how would you respond?
