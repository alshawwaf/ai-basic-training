## Key Points to Emphasise

1. **Lead with customer concern, not product name** — never open with "Let me tell you about Workforce AI Security." Open with the customer's pain: "Your employees are already using AI tools — do you know which ones?" The product is the answer to a question the customer is already asking.
2. **The Ninja knowledge IS the differentiator** — every competitor has a product datasheet. What participants have that competitors' SEs don't is the ability to explain WHY the technology works: what models detect prompt injection, how classification catches sensitive data, why traditional security misses LLM attacks. Technical depth builds trust.
3. **Demo over deck** — a 5-minute live demo of Workforce AI Security discovering shadow AI tools, or guardrails blocking a prompt injection in real time, is worth more than 30 slides. Always push to show, not tell. The hands-on lab experience from Sessions 5.2 and 5.3 prepares participants to run these demos confidently.

## Common Questions and Answers

**Q: What if the customer hasn't started with AI yet?**
A: They have — they just don't know it. Employees are using ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, and dozens of other AI tools through browsers and personal accounts. The discovery capability in Workforce AI Security proves this. Position the first conversation as an assessment: "Let us show you what's already happening in your environment." The data always tells a compelling story.

**Q: How do I handle a customer who only wants one product (e.g., just guardrails)?**
A: Start where the pain is. If they only need guardrails today, sell guardrails today. But during the engagement, ask: "Who in your organisation is using AI tools beyond this application?" and "Are any of your teams building AI agents?" These discovery questions naturally expand the conversation to the full platform. Land and expand is the right motion — never force a customer into a bundle they don't need yet.

**Q: How do I position against free open-source tools?**
A: Open-source guardrail libraries (Guardrails AI, NeMo Guardrails, LLM Guard) are building blocks, not solutions. They require engineering effort to deploy, maintain, and update. They don't come with management consoles, audit logging, compliance reporting, or enterprise support. The positioning is the same as Linux vs enterprise Linux, or OpenSSL vs a managed TLS service: the technology might be similar, but the operational overhead and enterprise readiness are the differentiators. Ask: "Who on your team will maintain and update the open-source guardrails when new attack techniques emerge next month?"

## Facilitator Notes

- This is the final session of the entire AI Ninja Program. End with energy. The role-plays should feel like a graduation exercise, not a test. Encourage participants to be creative and challenge each other.
- During role-plays, watch for participants who default to feature-listing instead of leading with pain. Gently redirect: "You started with the product. Try again — start with the customer's problem."
- The program wrap-up should be celebratory. Acknowledge the journey: 15 weeks, from foundational ML to building AI applications to positioning AI security. Participants have a rare combination of technical depth and sales acumen.
- Connect to the capstone explicitly: graduates will demo both their Stage 4 RAG assistant (showing they can build AI) and Check Point AI Security (showing they can secure AI). This dual capability is what makes them AI Ninjas.
- If time permits, ask each participant to share their single biggest takeaway from the entire program. This creates closure and reinforces the learning.
