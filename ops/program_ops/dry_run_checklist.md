# Dry-Run Checklist — Session 0.1

> Run this session with 2-3 colleagues before the first real cohort. The goal is to find every gap that reading alone can't catch.

---

## 2 Weeks Before

- [ ] Recruit 2-3 volunteers (ideally mix of: one who knows AI, one who doesn't, one in a different time zone)
- [ ] Schedule a 75-minute slot (60 min session + 15 min debrief)
- [ ] Confirm video conferencing tool and screen sharing works
- [ ] Send volunteers the Session 0.1 README.md to pre-read (or don't — to simulate cold participants)

## 1 Week Before

- [ ] Do a complete read-through of the facilitator guide (guide_00_ai_landscape.md)
- [ ] Do a complete read-through of the discussion guide (01_ai_landscape/discussion_guide.md)
- [ ] Prepare your 1-2 personal examples of AI claims from the field
- [ ] Prepare the 5-question framework as a shareable visual (slide, whiteboard, or screen share)
- [ ] Test your screen sharing — can volunteers see the content clearly?
- [ ] Prepare a backup plan if the video call drops (phone bridge, reschedule window)

## Day of — Before the Session

- [ ] Open all materials: README.md, discussion_guide.md, facilitator guide
- [ ] Have a timer visible (phone timer or on-screen) — you WILL lose track of time
- [ ] Have a notepad ready for observations: what worked, what was confusing, where you ran over time
- [ ] Mute notifications — treat this like the real thing

## During the Session — Track These

### Timing
- [ ] Did the welcome + intro fit in 5 minutes?
- [ ] Did the AI terminology section take more or less than 10 minutes?
- [ ] Did the security stack map section feel rushed or padded?
- [ ] Did the AI washing / 5-question framework get enough time?
- [ ] Did the round-table exercise get at least 15 minutes?
- [ ] Did you finish within 60 minutes? If not, what ran over?

### Content
- [ ] Was the 4-layer AI hierarchy (AI → ML → DL → GenAI) clear on first explanation?
- [ ] Did volunteers understand the 5-question framework without re-reading?
- [ ] Were any terms undefined or confusing? (Note which ones)
- [ ] Was the discussion guide's Exercise 1 (round-table) easy to facilitate?
- [ ] Did volunteers have enough to say, or did the discussion stall?

### Engagement
- [ ] Did volunteers participate, or did you lecture for 60 minutes?
- [ ] Which discussion prompt generated the most conversation?
- [ ] Which section felt least engaging? Why?
- [ ] Did anyone check out (camera off, multitasking)?

### Logistics
- [ ] Could everyone see shared content clearly?
- [ ] Did screen transitions between materials feel smooth?
- [ ] Was the session flow (guide timing table) easy to follow while presenting?

## After the Session — 15-Minute Debrief

Ask your volunteers:
1. "What was the single most useful thing you heard?"
2. "What was confusing or unclear?"
3. "Was the pace too fast, too slow, or right?"
4. "Would you want to continue to Session 0.2?"
5. "What would you change?"

## After the Debrief — Your Action Items

- [ ] Review your timing notes — adjust the facilitator guide time blocks if needed
- [ ] Fix any content gaps or confusing sections you identified
- [ ] Update discussion prompts if any fell flat
- [ ] Note which personal examples landed best — use those for the real session
- [ ] Decide: Am I ready to run this for the cohort, or do I need another dry-run?

---

## Success Criteria

You're ready for the real cohort when:

- You can deliver the session in 55-65 minutes without rushing the round-table
- You can explain the 5-question framework from memory, not reading it
- At least one volunteer said something like "I didn't know that" or "I'll use that"
- You know which personal examples work best
- You have a plan for when discussion stalls (backup prompts, call on people by name)
