# AI Ninja Program — Half-Page Brief

**For:** Head of Security Engineering &nbsp;·&nbsp; **From:** Khalid Alshawwaf

---

**The problem.** Every customer conversation now has AI in it — vendors pitching "AI-powered EDR," CISOs asking us to evaluate LLM use cases, competitors claiming their anomaly detection is "machine learning." Our engineers are world-class at firewalls and SIEM, but the AI layer is increasingly what decides who wins the deal. The gap is not awareness — it's technical depth, and that depth has to be built deliberately.

**The proposal.** A 15-week, cohort-based internal training program that takes a security engineer with zero ML background and turns them into someone who can explain AI to a customer, evaluate vendor claims with technical credibility, build working ML classifiers for security use cases, critique neural network architectures, and demo a live AI security assistant. Built around one principle: **people learn AI by manipulating it, not reading about it** — every lesson is hands-on Python against real or realistic security data.

**The structure.** 21 lessons across 5 stages, in 3 progressive certification tiers so people can stop where they need to:

| Tier | Weeks | Capability |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — AI Foundations** | 1–7 | Can explain AI/ML to a customer and evaluate vendor AI claims |
| **2 — AI Practitioner** | 8–10 | Can prototype a neural network and assess AI architectures |
| **3 — AI Ninja** | 11–15 | Can build AI-powered security tools and lead AI engagements |

Stages cover: AI positioning → classic ML → intermediate ML → neural networks → generative AI / RAG → applied Check Point AI products.

**Why it will work.** Three things separate this from "watch a Coursera playlist": (1) **graded assessment gates** between stages — quiz, mini-project, architecture review, live capstone demo — so people can't just run solution files and graduate; (2) **real security data** in capstones — real CVEs, real phishing URLs, MITRE ATT&CK — so graduates walk away with something demo-able; (3) **every Ninja graduate ships a portable RAG security assistant** they can pull out in any customer meeting.

**The ask.** Endorsement to recruit a pilot cohort of 12–16 Security Engineers/Architects across the Americas. 5–8 hours/week per participant for 15 weeks. Sponsorship to position participation as career investment. A review panel (you + 2–3 stakeholders) for the final capstone presentations.

**What success looks like.** A first cohort of 12–16 certified engineers who can hold technical AI conversations with any customer, each carrying a working AI security demo on their laptop, with a repeatable curriculum ready for the next cohort. The customers we sell to are judging us on AI fluency. This is the lowest-risk, highest-leverage way to build that fluency across the team — using internal time, internal expertise, and a curriculum that's already built and tested.
