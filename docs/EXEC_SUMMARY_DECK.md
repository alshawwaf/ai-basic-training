## Slide 2 — The problem

# Every customer conversation now has AI in it

- Vendors pitching "AI-powered EDR" and "ML-driven threat detection"
- CISOs asking us to evaluate their LLM and RAG use cases
- Competitors claiming their anomaly detection is "machine learning"
- Customers expecting us to engage on AI as **peers, not observers**

**Our engineers are world-class at firewalls, SIEM, and EDR.**
**The AI layer is increasingly the part that decides who wins the deal.**

> *Speaker note:* "The gap is not awareness — everybody knows AI is important. The gap is technical depth. Reading a blog about transformers does not let you push back when a vendor says 'our model catches zero-days' and you need to ask which model, trained on what data, with what false-positive rate."

## Slide 3 — The proposal

# A 15-week internal training program

**Takes a security engineer with zero ML background and turns them into someone who can:**

- **Explain** AI/ML to a customer in plain language
- **Evaluate** vendor AI claims with technical credibility
- **Build** working ML classifiers for security use cases
- **Critique** neural network architectures
- **Demo** a live AI-powered security assistant
- **Position** Check Point AI Security products with technical authority

**One operating principle:** People learn AI by manipulating it, not reading about it. Every lesson is hands-on Python against real or realistic security data.

> *Speaker note:* "The whole curriculum is already built and tested. 21 lessons, hundreds of pages of hands-on labs, real datasets — not slides. I can demo the portal after this."

---

## Slide 4 — How it's structured

# 21 lessons · 5 stages · 3 progressive certification tiers

| Tier | Weeks | What graduates can do |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — AI Foundations** | 1–7 | Hold credible AI conversations with customers; evaluate vendor claims |
| **2 — AI Practitioner** | 8–10 | Prototype neural networks; assess AI solution architectures |
| **3 — AI Ninja** | 11–15 | Build AI-powered security tools; lead AI engagements end-to-end |

**5 stages:** AI Positioning → Classic ML → Intermediate ML → Neural Networks → Generative AI / RAG → Applied Check Point AI

> *Speaker note:* "The tier system matters because not everyone needs the full depth. A senior architect who only needs sales credibility can stop at Tier 1 in 7 weeks. Engineers building tools go all the way to Tier 3. We get more participation because we respect their time."

## Slide 5 — Why it will work

# Three things that separate this from "watch a Coursera playlist"

**1. Graded assessment gates between stages**
Quiz · mini-project · architecture review · live capstone demo
*People can't just run solution files and graduate — they have to demonstrate they understood it.*

**2. Real security data, not toy datasets**
Real CVE advisories · real phishing URLs · MITRE ATT&CK · packet captures
*Capstones use data graduates could demo to a customer the next day.*

**3. Every Ninja graduate ships a portable demo**
A laptop-ready RAG security assistant pre-loaded with curated threat intel + a 5-minute customer-facing script
*The deliverable is not a certificate — it's a sales tool.*

> *Speaker note:* "The capstone is the part I'm most excited about. Imagine an engineer walking into a customer meeting, pulling out their laptop, and saying 'let me show you what I built in our AI program.' That's worth 10x a certificate."

## Appendix — Deck delivery notes

**For the presenter (Khalid):**

- **Pacing:** Aim for ~45 seconds per slide. Slides 2 and 5 are the most important — slow down on those. Slide 6 (the ask) should be deliberate, not rushed.
- **What to bring:** Laptop with the portal running locally (`http://localhost:5000`). After Slide 7, offer to demo the home page (showing the neural-network mesh roadmap), one interactive lesson (e.g. Lesson 1.1's Explore mode), and one capstone deliverable (the RAG security assistant). This is the moment that converts the proposal from "interesting idea" to "I get it."
- **Anticipated questions:**
  - *"What's the expected ROI?"* → Frame in deal-cycle terms: one technical engineer who can confidently engage a customer's AI question can shorten a deal cycle or unblock a stalled one. The cost is internal time; the upside is faster, higher-credibility customer engagements.
  - *"Why not use an external vendor like Coursera or DataCamp?"* → External courses teach generic ML. This program teaches AI through the lens of security use cases (phishing, intrusion detection, malware visualisation, CVE retrieval) and ends with a Check Point–specific positioning module. No external vendor offers that.
  - *"Why 15 weeks? Can we go faster?"* → The tier system already supports this. Tier 1 is 7 weeks for engineers who only need customer credibility. Tier 2 is 10 weeks for engineers who need architecture-review depth. The full 15 is only for the engineers we want building tools.
  - *"Who teaches it?"* → Self-paced modules + one weekly live Q&A session. The live sessions can be run by me or rotated across senior participants — defer to your preference.
  - *"What if a participant drops out?"* → Tier system protects against this. Even if someone only completes Tier 1, they walk away with real customer-conversation capability. There's no "all or nothing" failure mode.
