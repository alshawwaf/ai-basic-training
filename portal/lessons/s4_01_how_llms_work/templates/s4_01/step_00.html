A Large Language Model is a next-token predictor

An LLM — short for Large Language Model — is the kind of system that powers ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Despite the name, its only job is surprisingly narrow: given the words so far, predict what word comes next. One token at a time. Everything else you've heard about — reasoning, coding, summarisation — emerges from doing this prediction loop billions of times on trillions of words of text.

Type a sentence below and watch it happen. The next-token guess appears to the right of the last word.

Predicted next tokens

The loop: predict next token → append it → predict again → append → repeat. This is how LLMs generate entire paragraphs from a short prompt. Each token is chosen based on all previous tokens.
