# Discussion Guide — Session 0.2: Competitor Analysis

> **For facilitators and self-study.** Use these exercises during the live session or work through them independently.

---

## Exercise 1: Vendor AI Teardown (15 min)

Pick a vendor from Session 0.2 that you encounter most often in competitive deals. Without looking at the session notes, try to answer:

1. What is their primary AI/ML approach? (supervised, unsupervised, LLM, hybrid)
2. What specific ML techniques are they likely using?
3. What is their biggest genuine AI strength?
4. What is their most exploitable AI limitation?
5. What question would you ask in a competitive bake-off to expose that limitation?

**Check your answers against the session notes. What did you miss?**

## Exercise 3: Translate the Datasheet (10 min)

Read the following (fictional but realistic) vendor datasheet excerpt and translate every AI claim into plain technical language:

> "Our next-generation AI platform leverages proprietary deep learning models trained on over 500 billion security events. Our self-learning behavioral engine creates a unique digital fingerprint for every user and device, enabling autonomous detection and response to sophisticated threats in real-time. Our AI SOC analyst processes alerts 50x faster than human analysts."

## Exercise 4: The Positioning Role-Play (10 min)

**Setup:** One person plays a customer who just saw a compelling demo from [choose a vendor]. The other person is the architect positioning against that vendor.

**Customer opening:** "We just saw [vendor]'s demo and their AI looks really impressive. They showed us [specific feature]. Why should we look at your solution instead?"

**Rules:**
- Don't attack the competitor's AI — acknowledge what's real
- Pivot to the limitation that matters most for THIS customer's environment
- Ask a question that makes the customer think differently about the competitive AI claim
- Reference specific ML concepts when appropriate

**Rotate roles after 5 minutes.**

---

## Exercise 5: Your Own Product's AI (5 min)

Apply the same analysis framework to your own product or solution:

1. What AI/ML does your product actually use? (Be precise — not marketing language)
2. What training data does it use? How often is it retrained?
3. What are the genuine AI strengths?
4. Where does the AI fall short or require human oversight?
5. If a competitor applied the 5-question framework from Session 0.1 to YOUR product, where would you be weakest?

**This is the most important exercise.** You cannot credibly evaluate competitors if you cannot honestly evaluate yourself.

---

## Self-Study Reflection Questions

1. Which vendor's AI approach most closely resembles your own? What can you learn from how they position it?
2. Which competitive limitation do you most often see confirmed in customer environments?
3. If you could only ask one question in a competitive bake-off to differentiate on AI, what would it be?
4. How would you explain the difference between supervised ML (CrowdStrike/SentinelOne) and unsupervised ML (Darktrace) to a non-technical buyer?
